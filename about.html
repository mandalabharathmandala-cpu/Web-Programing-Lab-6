About HAL

Eight decades of aerospace excellence and innovation

HAL Headquarters, Bengaluru

Our Legacy

India's Aerospace Pioneer

Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL) is India's premier public sector aerospace and defence company. Headquartered in Bengaluru, Karnataka, we specialize in designing, manufacturing, and overhauling combat aircraft, helicopters, unmanned aerial vehicles, jet engines, avionics, and defense hardware.

With 11 dedicated R&D centers and 21 manufacturing divisions across 4 production units, HAL operates as one of Asia's largest aerospace companies with an annual turnover exceeding US$3 billion.

Our Mission

To be a world-class aerospace and defence enterprise, committed to enhancing self-reliance in defence and meeting the requirements of the Armed Forces through indigenous design, development, manufacture, and customer support.

Our Vision

To become a leading global aerospace and defence company, providing advanced technology solutions, achieving excellence in quality, and maintaining our position as the backbone of India's aerospace industry.

Journey Through Time

Our Milestones

1940

Foundation

Hindustan Aircraft Limited established by Walchand Hirachand in association with Maharaja of Mysore

1942

Production Begins

Started manufacturing Harlow PC-5, Curtiss P-36 Hawk, and Vultee A-31 Vengeance aircraft

1957

Jet Engine Era

Commenced manufacturing Bristol Siddeley Orpheus jet engines under license

1964

HAL Formation

Hindustan Aeronautics Limited officially formed through merger and consolidation

1967

HF-24 Marut

First indigenous fighter-bomber aircraft manufactured, marking India's entry into combat aircraft design

1980s

Expansion Phase

Rapid growth with licensed production of MiG-21, MiG-27, SEPECAT Jaguar, and other aircraft

2001

HAL Dhruv Launch

Advanced Light Helicopter enters service, showcasing indigenous design capabilities

2016

HAL Tejas IOC

Light Combat Aircraft achieves Initial Operational Clearance for Indian Air Force

2024

Maharatna Status

Awarded prestigious Maharatna status, gaining enhanced operational autonomy

2025

ISRO Collaboration

Received complete Small Satellite Launch Vehicle technology from ISRO, first full rocket tech transfer
